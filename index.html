Quick Tips & Mindset Hacks
  • Balance AO: AO1 (30%), AO2 (35%), AO3 (35%).
  • Time: Section A – 30min; B – 45min; C – 45min.
  • Formula stash: PED, YED, elasticity formulas.
  • Ritual: 5 deep breaths & “I’ve got this” mantra.
  • Pre-exam: 7–8h sleep, protein breakfast, 10-min light review.
Section A: Data Response
  • Step 1: Read & annotate—underline keywords & data trends.
  • Step 2: Define 2–3 key terms (exact syllabus language).
  • Step 3: Quote figures & calculate % changes precisely.
  • Step 4: Draw annotated diagram—shifts, equilibrium, welfare areas.
  • Step 5: Analysis chain—“Because … therefore … leading to …”.
  • Step 6: Evaluation—3 bullets: data limits, assumptions, alt factors.
  • Step 7: Link back—summarize welfare/equity impact.
Section B: Micro Essays
  • Structure: Intro → Diagram → Analysis → Eval → Conclusion.
  • Intro starters: “This essay examines …”, “Economists define …”.
  • Key diagrams: tax incidence, subsidy, PED curves.
  • Analysis: link theory to real-world (e.g., market intervention).
  • Eval phrases: “However …”, “Alternatively …”, “A possible drawback …”.
  • Conclusion: succinctly weigh pros & cons.
Section C: Macro Essays
  • Context: political lag, import leakages, informal sector.
  • Diagrams: AD/AS shifts, Keynesian LRAS.
  • Global: commodity price shocks, exchange rate changes.
  • Conclusion: compare policies & recommend per context.
